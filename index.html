A little history

The shophouses of Haji Lane have a history no less interesting than the stores they house now. In the 60s and 70s, the area provided lodging for poor Malay families, and also gave shelter to pilgrims on their annual pilgrimage to Mecca. Incidentally, that’s where the name of the street has its roots — ‘Haji’ is the Malay word for a Muslim man who’s completed a pilgrimage to Mecca.

Get a photo (or two) with iconic murals

Walking down Haji lane reveals walls that aren’t just painted in vibrant colours but adorned with pro level street art!
Get inspired by the colourful aztec design, recognisable portraits, and don't be suprised of you find yourself spending an hour or two gazing at these artworks.

Bonus tip: Kampong Glam truly comes alive during Ramadan (Muslim fasting month) leading up to Hari Raya Aidilfitri! Join in the iftar (breaking of fast) in the evenings, or stroll through the bustling night markets.
